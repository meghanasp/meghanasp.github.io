ðŸ“š Key Payment Ecosystem Roles Explained

  • Customer: The user who initiates the payment by purchasing a product or service.
  • Merchant: The business or individual selling the goods or services online or offline.
  • Biller: The entity to whom the customer owes payment. Often the same as the merchant, but could be a third-party service like a utility provider.
  • Payment Interface: The front-end UI the customer interacts with, like a checkout page or UPI app.
  • Payment Gateway: A secure service that encrypts and forwards transaction information from the customer to the payment processor or acquiring bank.
  • Payment Processor: The backend service that routes the transaction through the card network to the appropriate bank.
  • Card Network: Networks like Visa, MasterCard, or RuPay that facilitate communication between banks and define transaction rules.
  • Issuer Bank: The customerâ€™s bank that issues the debit/credit card and authorizes the transaction.
  • Acquirer Bank: The merchantâ€™s bank that receives the transaction funds after processing.
  • Provider: A bundled service provider that may offer gateway, processor, and settlement support (e.g., Razorpay, Stripe, PayPal).
